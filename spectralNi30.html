What is Spectral Analysis?

Spectral analysis uses satellite imagery across multiple wavelengths (spectral bands) to extract information about vegetation, soil, water, and atmospheric properties.

üîç How It Works

Satellites capture electromagnetic radiation reflected from Earth's surface across different wavelengths. Different materials (vegetation, water, soil) reflect different amounts of radiation at different wavelengths. We analyze these reflectance patterns to extract agricultural insights.

üåü Why It Matters

Traditional RGB imagery shows only what our eyes see. Spectral analysis reveals hidden patterns in the infrared, near-infrared, and short-wave infrared bands that directly correlate with crop health, moisture content, and biomass.

Understanding Spectral Bands

Different spectral bands capture different information. Here are the key bands used in agricultural analysis:

Red Band 0.6 - 0.7 ¬µm

What it captures: Visible red light reflected by vegetation

Agricultural use: Indicates vegetation presence and general plant vigor

Green Band 0.5 - 0.6 ¬µm

What it captures: Visible green light reflected by leaves

Agricultural use: Detects chlorophyll content and leaf pigmentation changes

NIR Band (Near-Infrared) 0.78 - 0.90 ¬µm

What it captures: Infrared radiation reflected by plant cells

Agricultural use: Most sensitive to biomass and leaf area. Critical for vegetation indices

SWIR Band (Short-Wave IR) 1.55 - 1.75 ¬µm

What it captures: Short-wave infrared radiation

Agricultural use: Indicates water content in vegetation and soil moisture

Key Vegetation Indices

Indices are mathematical combinations of spectral bands that highlight specific vegetation characteristics.

1

NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index)

Formula: (NIR - Red) / (NIR + Red)

Range: -1 to +1 (Higher = Healthier)

Use: Most widely used metric for vegetation health. Values > 0.7 indicate very healthy vegetation.

2

GNDVI (Green NDVI)

Formula: (NIR - Green) / (NIR + Green)

Range: -1 to +1

Use: More sensitive to chlorophyll content. Better for detecting stress in early stages.

3

SAVI (Soil-Adjusted Vegetation Index)

Formula: ((NIR - Red) / (NIR + Red + L)) * (1 + L)

Range: -1 to +1

Use: Corrects for soil background influence, better for sparse vegetation.

4

NDMI (Normalized Difference Moisture Index)

Formula: (NIR - SWIR) / (NIR + SWIR)

Range: -1 to +1

Use: Monitors water stress and drought conditions in crops.
